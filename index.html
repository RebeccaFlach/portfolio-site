    • Hi, I'm Beck!

I hope to apply my skills in engineering and human centered design to help build more sustainable, equitable, and enjoyable cities. I am especially interested in transportation and housing.     Check out some of my projects below, or get in touch!

    Coding

    I've been coding for nearly five years, starting with teaching myself web development in high school. In that time, I've worked on a variety of personal projects, internships, class work, and tutoring.

    Skills

    Clew

    I spent a summer working for Olin's OCCAM Lab on Clew, an app for indoor navigation for blind and visually impaired people.

    I focused reducing mapping error by integrating multiple data sources. We took odometry and Google Cloud Anchor data from multiple mapping sessions, and used graph optimization to create a more accurate map.

    Most of my work was done in Python, and I also became proficient in Swift and learned a lot about accessible design.

    MBTA Data Analysis

    As a project for my data science class, I worked on a data analysis of transportation equity. Using R, we combined census demographic data with MBTA ridership and location data to analyze access to the T. Although it was a simplistic approach that missed much of the nuance of equity issues, I learned a lot about Boston and issues with the T.

    You can view our report and code here.

    Flybox & Kite

    The other was Kite, building an app for financial education. Working in React Native, I set up an OAuth authentication flow, implemented other smaller systems as needed, and advised on other technical aspects of the project.

    Volpe Center: MSSIS

    At my summer internship at the DOT Volpe Center, I worked on the MSSIS maritime domain awareness system. I assisted with converting an outdated data pipeline to a Rust based system, and built a new error monitoring web interface. I learned a lot about networks and working with realtime data.

    MREN

    I started with small bug fixes, and as I learned I was given bigger projects to work on, and more freedom to make design and architecture decisions. I was taught how to write quality code, accept feedback through code reviews, make time estimates, use git, and work within a larger project.

    Shy Sioban

    In principes of integrated engineering, I worked in a team of four to create an interactive animatronic octopus. I was the primary person doing electrical and software work, which involved designing and setting up the system of eleven servo motors and an IR distance sensor controlled by an Arduino. I then wrote the code that controlled the servos in a way that produced fluid and interesting movement, and reacted to sensor input. I also contributed to mechanical design by designing the sensor housing.

    HVAC Analysis

    In one of my current classes, I am working to analyze the environmental and financial impact of energy recover ventilators in the Olin dorm HVAC system. This involves using thermodynamic modelling to predict energy savings in a variety of situations, understanding electricity pricing to predict potential cost savings, and researching the impact of ERVs on indoor air conditions. We also plan to repair currently broken ERVs at Olin and use that to inform a recommendation to the college about the benefits and costs of repairing the whole system.

    Other

    Pedestrian Safety

    For a recent class I took, Urban Sociology and Policy, I wrote an essay examining pedestrian safety in my hometown of Tucson, Arizona. I found that the city's poor pedestrian safety record is the result of the design of the roads and lack of pedestrian infrastructure, which in turn is a result of historical planning decisions and current policy priorities and culture. I learned a lot about the history of traffic engineering and new solutions.

    Read the full essay here.

    Collaborative Design

    In this human centered design class, I worked to design solutions for hospice workers and other people who work in end of life care. In the first phase, we interviewed ten people to learn about their experience in the field and what issues they face. In the second phase, we took synthesized our learnings, conducted further interviews and co-design sessions, and created a proposal for a education program for families to help change cultural views of death and hospice.
